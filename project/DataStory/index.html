Evolution of Music

December 19, 2017 by Max Premi and Maximilan Rünz

The music world is constantly increasing with lot of songs published everyday from all over the world. Moreover, the world is becoming more connected and this helps to spread music popularity.

We want to understand how music features evolved in the last roughly 60 years, as music is a really important social factor. People express themselves, and lot of cultural events and customs are derived from it. This change could help explain some movements influence, the creation of some music styles and what makes a song/genre popular. We will use the dataset from MillionSongDB, coupled with some website such as The Echo Nest API (now Spotify) and musicbrainz.

Music is a form of art, and a cultural activity that have been practiced since prehistoric time. Nowadays it is everywhere, and seems inseparable from our life-style, as even humming or hitting an object with your finger makes "music". The evolution of this field is thus a really intersting subject to study. It is linked to cultural movement in history, such as the 80's with hippies movement for example but also architecture (Baroque) or religion (Jean-Sébastien Bach was indeed doing muisc link to religion). This can help us understand how music evolves with the time with how events and movement influence it, but also how music affects these different things. It has also become a big industry, with the apparition of the term "commercial music", and interest more and more people, for example this video, that collected more than 100 millions views. In this study we will concentrate on music from the 20th century and beginning of 21th century, and we will try to awnser the following questions:

  • How did music features change during the last 80 years, in terms of duration, style, or even rythm ?
  • What makes a Song popular a few years ago, what make one today, and is the formula for a popularity possible to elaborate ? (all in function of genre distribution and not hotness in features)
We will try to awnser these with as much precision as possible in the following lines, while keeping in mind that evolution is a neutral term, and also that popularity is quite subjective.

Evolution of Music in General

We will concentrate in this section, in the evolution of music in general, meaning we did not do difference between genre of music yet. First we can introduce the first numbers. We had a total of 462546 songs at the beggining, and after cleaning (drop where they are to much undefined features or no year), we have aroud 220000 songs. The major features used are the following : the Tempo, Duration, Loudness, Terms, TermsFrequency and Termsweight. Hotness was not used for song as too much of them had a 0 one. We thus tried to map popularity in function of the genre distribution through the years. For the evolution of music we had really good result matching historical events, such as the loudness increase, which is a known problem since the 90s. Also change in Tempo with also increase and decrease, and finally duration with high increase from 1950. These results are detailled in the following section with more analysis and explanations. The Terms with frequency were used to map song to Parent Genre. We did the mapping ourselves and we achieve an accuracy of 75% which is quite correct. We also did a clustering (k-NN) of song to see if we could match the time period of a song, and we did get a result of 55% which is not that good. It is in the original work of the notebook, if you're interested in it.

Evolution of Tempo

Let's look at the Tempo evolution. We can clearly see that there has been an increase in the overall tempo since the 60s. With a little wave that converged to a value around 125. This will be discribed step by step by using the slider.


Evolution of Loudness

Here we do not need to explain it, we clearly see that the Loudness increased, but why is that and how did the time influence this ? First Loudness is defined as follow : "That attribute of auditory sensation in terms of which sounds can be ordered on a scale extending from quiet to loud.", or more theoritically, it is the subjective perception of sound pressure (which is is the local pressure deviation from the ambient (average, or equilibrium) atmospheric pressure, caused by a sound wave.)


Evolution of Duration

First we will discus the gap between 1960 (3 minutes average) to 1980 (3 minutes 45 average). But they have been a great increased on a short period, and this duration has been around the same since this period.


Evolution of Genre Population

We will talk here about the genre Distribution evolution. We can clearly see the trending genre moving with the time. So this is the evolution of music analyzed in function of the number of songs labelled by genre, we will now look at the features changes by genre. This evolution can be correlated to the popularity of a genre, as the more % it is, the more popular it is supposed to be (else it would not have been released) Also the Genre matching was done ourselves to reduce main genre to 14 genres. We used a Json found a website to reduce all the different tags to 28 genres, that we also reduce to the main 14 genres. The original tags were very variated and we treat almost 350 subgenres.


What we can infer

From this we can understand the popularity of some old time such as Jazz, Blues or Folk has greatly reduce since the begining of the 20 th century. This is due to the apparition of new genre based on this previous one. Also cultural movement or apparition of some famous people increased the popularity of a genre with the example of Bob Marley for the Reggae or the rise of Metal/ Heavy-Metal in the late 70s. And finally the technological advance have helped some genre such as Electronic to spread faster and take a place in this genre population.

Evolution of Loudness

The evolution of Loudness by genre confort our observation of Loudness War shown in the previous plots. Indeed there is a clear augmentation of loudness, with a clear higher change for metal, that might be due to the use of distorsion and other procedure that increase the song force. We can however see 3 genres that are less affected which are Folk,Blues and Jazz. This should be due to the accoustic nature of these genre as it does not use as much electronical material as the other genres.

Mean

We can see that the variation of loudness clearly increased linearly, for all genres. One interesting fact is how they were spread from each other at the beginning, but then joined in 1985, and spread again in the beginning of the loudness War. We have not found matching events or information for this regrouping, but there are some interesting fact to notice. Downtempo and Electronic has a really linear increased, mostly due to their close relationship with technologic discovery. And also, Metal and Reggae were the one with the lowest variation , it might be linked to the fact that it Metal appeared later than the other genre and used a high Loudness since the beginning and Reggae used maybe less this loudness increased.

So if we study the evolution of Tempo by genre, most of them actually the same trend as the global one we described earlier, with some exceptions. It's indeed amazing how they almost perfectly match The Reggae has a trend to go a little higher in term of tempo, this is maybe due to the apparition of the Ska subgenre. The curve goes up to 128 instead of 125 and stay a little higher than the other genre. As we have seen previously, Metal is a real special genre with the tendancy to never match the other genres. So it's curve is like the Reggae one, but shifted even higher with a BPm that went to 132 and finish at almost 130
